About Us

The Mission

Debachanya Girl Child Empowerment Initiative is a nonprofit dedicated to providing basic education to females. It is situated in Minna, Niger State, Nigeria, and was started by Achanya Deborah E. in January 2019. This foundation is one whose aim is to help vulnerable population especially girls have basic, necessary access to education and source of livelihood.

Debachanya Girl Child Empowerment Initiative is a foundation whose mission is to provide underprivileged populations, particularly girls, with basic, required access to education and a source of income. Our mission is to mobilize non-school going girls, between ages 6 and 17 from economically and socially backward rural areas in Nigeria and put them firmly in track of literacy through quality primary and secondary education.

This mission supports the acceleration and the attainment of the 1 - 6 and 16 SDGs related to ending poverty and hunger, ensuring healthy lives for all, ensuring inclusive and quality education, integrating water hygeine and sanitation and to promote peace and inclusive society for sustainable development.

The Goals

The school dropout rate is high in Niger villages, and this is very severe in the case of girls. The girl child education is neglected by parents and violates the rights of the girl children. Due to the poverty and inacess to the eductaional instituitions, girl children are thus forced into child labour and child marriages.

Also the coastal villages are frequently affected by cyclones, floods and bandits and this led to damage of livelihood sources. Women lost their regular source of livelihoods i.e. fishing, agriculture, etc. These women therefore needs alternate sources of livelihood in order to support themselves and their family wellbeing.

The goal of the project is to address long term sustainable livelihood solutions through skill training on micro enterprises, soap making, dress making, decorations and events planning, small chops making and many more for 1500+ women to earn additional income for their families.

The adolescent girls also needs access to life skills, sex education, vocational traings like sewing, dress making, digital skills and computers for 2000+ adolescent girls to improve the quality of life and social dignity.
